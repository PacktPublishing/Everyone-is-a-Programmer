### 1. **User retention rate in the first week**

- **What it measures**: This metric measures whether a new user returns to your app within the following week after using it for the first time.
- **Why it matters**: This is the most important survival metric for an MVP. It directly tells us whether the product shows enough value to users (such as Li Jing and Wang Chen) in the first time, making them feel that "this thing is useful and worth opening again." A healthy weekly retention rate means our core value proposition holds true.

### 2. **Core Behavior Conversion Rate**

- **What to measure**: We can specifically define it as "**The proportion of new users who successfully complete at least one habit check-in within 3 days**".
- **Why it matters**: This metric goes beyond simply "opening the app"; it measures whether users actually participate in our core loop (create a habit -> complete a check-in). If this ratio is high, it means our product design is effective and users can easily move from "setting goals" to "taking action." This validates the "frictionless experience" we pursue for Wang Chen and the "clear execution path" we provide for Li Jing.

### 3. **Continuous recording startup rate**

- **What to measure**: We can define it as "**the proportion of active users who have at least one habit for 3 or more consecutive days**".
- **Why it matters**: "Streak" is the core incentive of our MVP. This metric directly measures whether the mechanism is effective. If users can keep checking in for more than 3 days, it usually means that they have felt the positive feedback and motivation brought by the continuous record, and our core motivation cycle has begun to take effect. This is especially critical for Li Jing, who is easily discouraged.
